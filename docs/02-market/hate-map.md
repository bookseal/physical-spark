# The "Hate" Map

> Method: mine **real negative reviews** of competitors → cluster the complaints into pain themes →
> every pain someone shouts about is a door we can walk through. What people *hate* about the incumbents
> is the sharpest map of where a newcomer wins.
> Researched 2026-07-07 from public reviews/forums (linked). These skew negative by nature — that's the point;
> we're reading the complaint bin, not measuring average satisfaction.

---

## Competitor A — Makeblock mBot (K-12 coding-robot kits)

The best-selling entry robot. Loved by many, but the recurring 1-star themes are strikingly consistent.

**🔴 Pain 1 — "The examples don't even work."**
Reviewers report the learning-site examples are built on Scratch blocks that don't function as shown; the robot
only runs if you nest commands under a special "mBot Program" brick, so standard tutorials fail out of the box.
→ [flashgamer review](https://flashgamer.com/blog/comments/makeblock-mbot-review) · [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/www.makeblock.com)

**🔴 Pain 2 — "Nobody answers."**
Long forum threads of unhappy customers go unanswered; support staff described as lacking basic knowledge of
their own product. Users can't figure out how to use the onboard peripherals (RGB LEDs, light sensor, buzzer, IR).
→ [flashgamer review](https://flashgamer.com/blog/comments/makeblock-mbot-review)

**🔴 Pain 3 — "Hardware quirks nobody warns you about."**
Remotes share one frequency, so robots in the same room confuse each other (a classroom nightmare); some motor
speed settings just fail silently until you guess a higher value.
→ [flashgamer review](https://flashgamer.com/blog/comments/makeblock-mbot-review)

*(Balance: not all hate — some reviewers praise responsive replacements, e.g. a free sensor swap. Sample is noisy.)*

## Competitor B — DJI RoboMaster S1 ($500+ programmable combat robot)

Beautiful hardware, cult following — and a cautionary tale about **closed ecosystems**.

**🔴 Pain 1 — "They abandoned it."**
Owners say DJI stopped meaningfully supporting/updating the S1, leaving an expensive product stranded.
→ [DJI Forum: "Disappointed DJI has outright abandoned the S1"](https://forum.dji.com/thread-258727-1-1.html)

**🔴 Pain 2 — "Promised upgrades that never came."**
Buyers point to marketing videos/images showing arm add-ons and expandability that never shipped — described
as misleading / false advertising.
→ [DJI Forum](https://forum.dji.com/thread-258727-1-1.html) · [SparkPilots thread](https://sparkpilots.com/threads/dji-robomaster-s1-reviews.14452/)

**🔴 Pain 3 — "Limited, for the price."**
A basic, limited command set with little real utility — after spending $1,000+ on unit and accessories, several
owners say they won't buy DJI again.
→ [SparkPilots thread](https://sparkpilots.com/threads/dji-robomaster-s1-reviews.14452/)

## Competitor C — Online robotics courses (Coursera / bootcamps)

Thinner review trail, but the shape of the complaint is familiar: you can **audit for free without graded
feedback**, and learners who go it alone tend to stall. Even well-liked courses (e.g. Northwestern's *Modern
Robotics*) leave you on your own between videos.
→ [Reddit discussion aggregator](https://reddsera.com/specializations/modernrobotics/)
